2009x12x02

  • Conversion of monitor stars to guide (or acq/guide) stars
    • Obs ID 11014 in the NOV3009 schedule included a monitor window in the OR in order to have ACA measurements of the target star (RR Lyr).
    • FOT Mission Planning converted the monitor star to an acq/guide star.
    • The FOT MP Tip/Trick used is "When and How to Convert a Monitor Window to a Guide Star". The criteria for conversion are:
      • The monitor star magnitude is brighter than or equal to 10.5
      • The monitor star would be chosen by SAUSAGE as a guide star in stage 3 or better
    • In this case, SAUSAGE chose the monitor star as an acq star in Stage 1, but did not actually choose it as a guide star because the guide search box (including dither) was 1 arcsec too close to an ACA quadrant boundary.
    • We have determined (from previous measurements) that impacts to onboard pointing control and ground aspect determination are generally not significant for guide stars near the quadrant boundaries.
    • The major advantage of monitor star conversion is that if the star is lost, the OBC will attempt reacquisition, whereas a monitor window would simply be lost for the duration of the observation.
    • We should consider revising the second criterion to say "The monitor star would be chosen by SAUSAGE as an acquisition star in stage 3 or better".
    • SS&AWG agreed to this modification.
